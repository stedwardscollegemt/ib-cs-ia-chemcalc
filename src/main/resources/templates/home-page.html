C = M/V

c: This represents the concentration of the solution and is measured in units such as moles per liter (mol/L) or grams per liter (g/L), depending on the context.

m: This stands for the mass of the solute, which is the substance being dissolved in the solution. The unit for mass can be grams (g) or any other appropriate unit of mass.

v: This represents the volume of the solution and is measured in liters (L) or any other suitable unit of volume.

To use the concentration equation, you need to ensure that the units of mass (m) and volume (v) are consistent.
